    OOSOOM

    see [out of sight, out of mind](#out of sight)

    Out of sight, out of mind.

    Prov. If you do not see someone or something frequently, you will forget about it. (Sometimes used to imply that you will forget about people who have moved away.)

    • She thought she’d miss her boyfriend when he went away, but it was out of sight,out of mind.
    • Ever since I moved, none of my old friends have gotten in touch with me. It’s out of sight, out of mind with them, evidently.

    The grass is always greener on the other side (of the fence).

    Prov. If you say the grass is always greener or the grass is always greener on the other side of the fence, you mean that other people often seem to be in a better situation than you, but in reality their situation may not be as good as it seems.

    • You know what it’s like — the grass is always greener on the other side of the fence. I’m always looking at jobs advertised online and thinking I’d be better off somewhere else.
    • The grass is always greener on the other side—the sooner you realize that and stop comparing your life to others’, the happier you’ll be!
